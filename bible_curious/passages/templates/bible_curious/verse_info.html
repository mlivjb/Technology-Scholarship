Sections of the Bible

The Law books are the first books of the Bible and are about the creation of the world, and the peoples rebellion against God. Humanity didnâ€™t trust God and so their relationship with him and with creation is broken.

The History Books cover a period of time when the nations of Israel were established and ruled by judges and then kings.

The Poetry books are about wisdom, through poems and stories, giving insights on trusting God through periods of sadness, joy, and teaching us about human nature.

The Prophets were social critics who reminded the Jewish people of God and his concern for the poor and the laws and promises God made for his people.

The Gospels follow the life, death and resurrection of Jesus. Each was written in a different perspective, and has different themes.

The book of Acts is about the history of the Church and its establishment, and how the word of God spread to other countries and places.

The Letters books are collections of letters written by prophets (mostly a guy called Paul) who wrote to places that needed to be reminded of God. It tells about how to live our lives as Christians.

The Book of Revelation is written as an apocalyptic style text of what's to come. It uses poetic and dramatic imagery which can be interpreted many different ways.
