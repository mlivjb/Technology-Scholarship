What is the Passover

Context

After all the plagues, God decided to send one final plague that would ensure that Pharaoh would drive out the Israelites completely.

The Jewish people celebrate the outcomes of this plague with the Festival of the Passover, so what was the plague and what did God tell the Israelites in order for them to be delivered?


Exodus 12

Plague of the Firstborn

The last plague that God sent to Egypt was the plague of the first born son. He told the Israelites through Moses that:

Exodus 12:4 - 7
“‘About midnight I will go throughout Egypt. Every firstborn son in Egypt will die, from the firstborn son of Pharaoh, who sits on the throne, to the firstborn son of the female slave, who is at her hand mill, and all the firstborn of the cattle as well.

There will be loud wailing throughout Egypt — worse than there has ever been or ever will be again. But among the Israelites not a dog will bark at any person or animal.’

Then you will know that the Lord makes a distinction between Egypt and Israel.”

However, God promised the Israelites that he would spare anyone who takes a lamb, pure of any defects, and looks after it a week before that night. Then, the evening of the plague, each household must kill the lamb, spread its blood on the doorframes, and eat the meat after cooking it over a fire, with bread that has no yeast.


That same night, the angel of the Lord passed through all of Egypt. Those that had smeared the blood of the lamb across their door frames were spared - and all members of the household were safe.

However, of those that did not go through with the offering, every first born son in the household died.

One of those people was Pharaoh's son. He summoned Moses and Aaron and sent them and all their people out of Egypt. He was very angry at Moses, and all the Israelites but he was mostly angry with God. So the Israelites got up quickly, gathered their things and left.


Why did God kill the Egyptians?

God chose to kill the Egyptians to punish them for their sense of security and power. For the Israelites endured hundreds of years of torture and punishment at the hands of Pharoah, yet they still worshipped God and had strong faith.

God tests the security of Egypt through sending the plagues as they are relying on Pharaoh and their money and power to prove themselves a strong nation.

By killing the firstborns God is showing his power and that he is almighty and able to do so. It brings glory and respect to God as Pharaoh was mocking the Israelites and the power that God holds.

By bringing this plague the Israelites are finally free from their oppression in Egypt. God shows his compassion for them, by bringing justice.


Why celebrate the Passover?

The passover is a celebration of God's deliverance to the Israelites. It is a reminder of God's character of hope, protection and compassion, and that God saves people from their struggles.

Because the Israelites had such strong faith he rewarded them and was gracious to them.
