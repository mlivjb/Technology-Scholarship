Who is Moses?

Exodus 1

Origin

Around 3,500 years ago a group of people called the Israelites (from Israel) lived in Egypt as slaves. The Egyptian king called Pharaoh was corrupt and sought power.

When he realised the Iraelities were becoming too numerous in his land he sent out an order to kill all the Israelite baby boys under two in the land.

Exodus 2:1-10

Birth of Moses

However an Iraelite mother placed her baby boy in a basket, covered it with tar so that it would float down the river. She did this in hopes that her son would be saved from the hand of Pharaoh on her people, and give him a chance at life.

The basket travelled down the river and made it to the Egyptian palace where the Egyptian princess found the basket, with the baby inside. Having pity she took him in as her own and called him 'Moses' which is Hebrew for "I drew him out of the water."

Exodus 2:6
She opened it and saw the baby. He was crying, and she felt sorry for him. “This is one of the Hebrew babies,” she said.
