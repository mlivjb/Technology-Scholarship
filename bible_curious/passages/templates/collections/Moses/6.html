Why was Moses called?

There are many reasons that God chose Moses as the one to deliver the Israelites from slavery.

Personal Connection

One of these was that Moses had a status in Israel as the Prince of Egypt. The Israelites knew him and respected him so they were more likely to listen to him.

This also goes along with how Moses grew up with Pharoah as by the time Moses turned 80 the Pharaohs would have turned over to the next in line. This proves a personal connection and Moses would be the most likely to persuade Pharaoh of the Israelites deliverance.

Trust

Another reason is that God was teaching Moses to trust and listen to him. In the last step we see how Moses was hesitant to obey God’s commandment to him due to his lack of interpersonal skills.

However God sends him anyway to show Moses that he can rely on God to give him the words he needs.

God can use anyone

God is also showing here that anyone is capable when they have God with them. God is not limited to those who are skillful and competent.

God is there for all. He uses Moses to show how anyone can be a vessel of God's love; his grace isn’t limited.

God gives us companions

Moses isn’t sent by God alone. He feels incompetent as one person but God sends him with his brother Aaron and has the support of the Israelites (around 2.5 - 3 million people).

God chose Moses to show what one person can achieve versus how much a whole community can achieve together.

God promises deliverance

In Genesis, the first book of the Bible, it is said that God promises the Israelites who are enslaved that He will save them and deliver them from their slavery.

God needed Moses as a vessel to help him achieve that mission. He worked through Moses in order to bring himself glory, and fulfil his promise to the Israelites.
